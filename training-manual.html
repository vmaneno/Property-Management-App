Chapter 1

System Overview & User Roles

The Property Management App is a browser-based platform that lets property owners, management agencies, and tenants collaborate on property administration — covering everything from unit occupancy to rent payment tracking — all from a single interface.

1.1 What the System Manages

  • Agencies — property management companies that oversee multiple clients.
  • Clients — property owners whose portfolios are managed by an agency.
  • Properties — individual buildings or developments (apartments, villas, commercial units, etc.).
  • Property Units — individual rentable units within a property.
  • Tenants — occupants assigned to a specific unit with a rental agreement.
  • Transactions — financial entries (rent payments, deposits, charges) linked to tenants.

1.2 The Four User Roles

Access is controlled by four distinct roles, each with its own portal and colour theme:

RolePortal FileTheme ColourScope of Access
Tenant tenant.html Blue (#4361ee) Own unit, own transactions, own profile only
Client client.html Green (#2d9b59) Own properties, units, tenants & transactions
Agency agency.html Purple (#7209b7) All clients, properties and data under the agency
Master master.html Red (#d62828) Entire system — all agencies, clients, properties, transactions

1.3 How Data is Stored

All data is stored in your web browser's local storage. This means:

  • No internet connection is required after the files are opened.
  • Data is tied to the browser and device you use — switching browsers or devices will show a fresh database.
  • Your session (login state) is cleared automatically when you close the browser tab.
ℹ️
Demo Data Included The system ships with sample agencies, clients, properties, units, tenants, and 25 transactions so you can explore immediately without entering any data.

2.2 The Sidebar

Every portal after login has a fixed left sidebar. Click any item to switch to that view instantly — the page does not reload. The sidebar includes:

  • A logo/app title at the top.
  • Navigation links for each section (Dashboard, properties, tenants, etc.).
  • A Sign Out button at the bottom that clears your session and returns you to the login page.
💡
Mobile Users — On small screens the sidebar hides off-screen. Tap the hamburger (☰) icon at the top-left to open it, then tap a link or tap outside to close it.

2.3 Dark / Light Mode

The landing page has a theme toggle (moon/sun icon, top-right). Your preference is remembered in the browser across visits.

2.4 Modals (Pop-up Forms)

Add, edit, and view operations open a modal overlay on top of the current page. To close a modal without saving:

  • Click the ✕ button in the modal header, or
  • Click the Cancel / Back button in the footer.

2.5 Toast Notifications

After every save, delete, or error the system shows a small notification banner in the top-right corner. It disappears automatically after a few seconds. Colours indicate the type:

  • Green — success (record saved, deleted, uploaded).
  • Red — error (validation failure, permission denied).
  • Blue — informational (e.g. transaction reference number).
Chapter 3

Login, Password Setup & Recovery

Open index.html in your browser. You will see four rotating login cards — one for each role. Each card has three tabs:

  • Login — for existing users with credentials.
  • First Time Setup — for new accounts that have never logged in.
  • Forgot Password — to reset a forgotten password.

3.1 Standard Login

Tenant Login

  1. On the Tenant card, click Login.
  2. Enter your Property Code (e.g. PR001).
  3. Enter your Username and Password.
  4. Click Sign In. You are taken to your dashboard.

Client, Agency & Master Login

  1. Click the relevant card's Login tab.
  2. Enter your Username and Password.
  3. Click Sign In.

3.2 First Time Setup (New Account)

When an administrator creates a new Client, Agency, or Tenant account, the account has no username or password yet. The new user must complete First Time Setup.

  1. On the correct role card, click the First Time Setup tab.
  2. Enter your account code (e.g. Client Code, Agency Code, or Tenant ID).
  3. Enter the telephone number registered on your account — this proves your identity.
  4. Choose a username (must be unique).
  5. Choose a password (minimum 6 characters) and confirm it.
  6. Click Set Credentials. Your login is activated — use Login tab going forward.
⚠️
Telephone Must Match — The phone number you enter must exactly match what your administrator registered for your account. Contact your administrator if it does not match.

3.3 Forgot Password

  1. Click the Forgot Password tab on the relevant role card.
  2. Phase 1 — Enter your username and account code, then click Send Reset Code. Your email client opens with a pre-filled message containing a 6-digit code.
  3. Note the 6-digit code shown on screen (or in the email draft).
  4. Phase 2 — Enter the reset code, your new password, and confirm it.
  5. Click Reset Password. You are redirected to the Login tab.
⚠️
Code Expiry — The reset code is valid for 15 minutes only. If it expires, start the process again.

3.4 Signing Out

Click Sign Out in the sidebar. Your session is cleared and you return to the login page. Sessions also clear automatically when you close the browser tab.

Chapter 4

Tenant Portal

The Tenant Portal gives residents a private space to monitor their account balance, update personal details, post rent payments, and view their full transaction history.

Sidebar sections: Dashboard · My Profile · Post Transaction · My Transactions · Sign Out

4.1 Dashboard & Account Balance

The dashboard is the first screen after login. It shows four summary cards at the top:

CardWhat it Shows
PropertyName of the property you occupy
Unit DetailsUnit number, bedrooms, bathrooms, floor
Move-in DateYour lease start date
Account BalanceRunning total of all your transactions

Below the cards is a Balance Card showing:

  • Your current account balance (green = credit/no amount due; red = debit/amount outstanding).
  • Your agreed rent amount and payment frequency.
  • Your deposit amount.

At the bottom, the last 5 transactions are listed with date, description, amount, status (Verified/Pending), and reference number.

4.2 My Profile

Click My Profile in the sidebar to view and edit your personal information.

Editing Your Profile

  1. Click My Profile.
  2. Update any of: First Name, Last Name, Telephone, Email, Address.
  3. To change your password, type a new password in the New Password field and confirm it. Leave both blank to keep your current password.
  4. Click Save Changes.
💡
Your Tenant Number (format: TN-###), Property, and Unit are read-only. Contact your Client or Agency administrator if these need correcting.

4.3 Posting a Transaction

Use this screen to submit a rent payment, deposit, or any financial entry for your account. All tenant-submitted transactions start as Pending until verified by your manager.

  1. Click Post Transaction in the sidebar.
  2. Enter the Date of the transaction.
  3. The Currency field is filled automatically from your property — do not change it.
  4. Enter a clear Description (e.g. Rent Payment — June 2026).
  5. Enter the Amount. Use a positive number for payments you are making; use a negative number for charges or adjustments (if instructed to do so).
  6. Add an optional Note for any additional context.
  7. Click Choose File and attach a supporting document (bank receipt, screenshot, etc.). Accepted formats: PDF, JPG, PNG, DOC, DOCX. This field is required.
  8. Click Submit Transaction. A toast notification shows your transaction reference number (e.g. TXN-00026) — note this for your records.
⚠️
Document Required — You cannot submit a transaction without attaching a supporting document. Always keep a digital copy of your payment receipt.

4.4 Viewing My Transactions

  1. Click My Transactions in the sidebar.
  2. A full table of all your transactions is shown: Date, Description, Amount (green = credit, red = debit), and Status.
  3. Click on any Amount to open a detail modal showing the reference number, notes, and a link to view the attached document.
ℹ️
Transactions with status Pending have been received but not yet reviewed by your manager. Verified means your manager has confirmed the entry.
Chapter 5

Client Portal

The Client Portal is used by property owners to manage their entire portfolio — properties, units, tenants, and financial transactions.

Sidebar sections: Dashboard · My Properties · Property Units · Tenants · Transactions · Sign Out

5.1 Dashboard

Four summary cards provide an instant snapshot:

  • Properties — number of properties you own.
  • Total Units — units across all your properties.
  • Active Tenants — tenants currently on active leases.
  • Occupied Units — occupied / total ratio.

Below, the last 10 transactions are shown with a colour-coded running balance (green if positive, red if negative).

5.2 Managing Properties

Adding a Property

  1. Click My Properties, then + Add Property.
  2. Complete the form (see field reference in Chapter 11).
  3. Upload an optional property picture.
  4. Click Save Property.

Editing a Property

  1. Find the property in the table and click the Edit (✏️) button.
  2. Modify the required fields in the modal.
  3. Click Save Changes.

Deleting a Property

  1. Click the Delete (🗑) button next to the property.
  2. Confirm the deletion in the dialog.
🚫
Cannot Delete with Active Tenants — The delete button is disabled if the property has active tenants. Deactivate or move all tenants first.

5.3 Managing Property Units

Adding a Unit

  1. Click Property Units, then + Add Unit.
  2. Select the Property from the dropdown.
  3. Enter Unit Number, Rent Amount, Deposit Amount, Bedrooms, Bathrooms, Floor, and Status (Occupied or Vacant).
  4. Click Save Unit.

Bulk Unit Upload

  1. Click Units Upload.
  2. Click Download Template to get the Excel template.
  3. Fill in the spreadsheet columns: PropertyCode, UnitNumber, RentAmount, DepositAmount, Bedrooms, Bathrooms, Floor, UnitStatus.
  4. Save the file and click Choose File to upload it (.xlsx, .xls, or .csv).
  5. Review the preview panel — valid rows are shown in green, errors in red with an explanation.
  6. Click Import X Unit(s) to import the valid rows.
💡
Only valid rows are imported. Rows with errors are skipped — fix errors in the spreadsheet and re-upload for the skipped rows.

5.4 Managing Tenants

Adding a Tenant

  1. Click Tenants, then + Add Tenant.
  2. Fill in personal details: First Name, Last Name, ID Number, Telephone, Email, Address.
  3. Select the Property, then select the Unit (the unit list updates automatically).
  4. Set Rent Amount, Rent Frequency, Deposit Amount, and Move-in Date.
  5. Upload the three required documents:
    • Photograph — tenant photo (JPG/PNG)
    • ID Copy — government-issued ID scan (PDF/JPG/PNG)
    • Rent Agreement — signed lease (PDF/JPG/PNG/DOC/DOCX)
  6. Click Save Tenant. A Tenant Number (TN-###) is generated automatically.
⚠️
All three documents are required when adding a tenant manually. For bulk uploads, documents must be attached later via Edit Tenant.

Bulk Tenant Upload

  1. Click Tenants Upload and then Download Template.
  2. Fill the spreadsheet. Required columns: FirstName, LastName, IDNumber, Telephone, Email, Address, PropertyCode, UnitNumber, RentAmount, DepositAmount, RentFrequency, MoveInDate.
  3. Dates must be in YYYY-MM-DD format. RentFrequency must be one of: Monthly, Quarterly, SemiAnnually, Annually.
  4. Upload the file, review the validation preview, then click Import.
  5. After import, edit each tenant individually to attach their documents.

Editing a Tenant

Click Edit next to a tenant to update Name, Telephone, Email, Address, Rent Amount, Move-out Date, or Active status. Property and Unit cannot be changed via this form.

5.5 Managing Transactions

Posting a Single Transaction

  1. Click Transactions, then + Post Transaction.
  2. Select Property, then Tenant (tenant list updates automatically).
  3. Enter Date, Description, Amount (negative for debit/charge), and optional Note.
  4. Attach a supporting Document.
  5. Click Post Transaction.

Bulk Post Transactions

Use this to charge rent to multiple tenants in one action — ideal for month-end rent runs.

  1. Click Bulk Post.
  2. Enter the Date and select the Property.
  3. Choose Charge Type:
    • Monthly Rent — uses each tenant's individual rent amount automatically.
    • Custom Charge — enter a single amount applied to all selected tenants.
  4. Enter a Description (e.g. Rent — July 2026).
  5. In the tenant list, check the tenants to charge. Use Select All / None to speed this up.
  6. Click Post Transactions. One transaction is created per selected tenant.

Editing a Transaction

Click Edit next to any Pending (unverified) transaction to update its Date, Amount, Description, Note, or attached Document.

🚫
Verified transactions cannot be edited or deleted. Contact your Agency or Master Admin if a verified transaction needs correction.

Chapter 7

Master Admin Portal

The Master Admin has unrestricted access to every record in the system. This role is intended for the top-level administrator responsible for the platform as a whole.

Sidebar sections: Dashboard · Agencies · Clients · Properties · Units · Tenants · Transactions · Sign Out

7.1 Full System Dashboard

The Master dashboard shows aggregated statistics for the entire platform:

  • Total agencies, clients, properties.
  • Occupied / total units and active tenants.
  • Combined balance across all transactions system-wide.

Below are two tables: all agencies and the 10 most recent transactions across the whole system.

7.2 Agency Management

Adding an Agency

  1. Click Agencies, then + Add Agency.
  2. Enter a unique Agency Code, Name, Address, Telephone, and Email.
  3. Set Type: Standard for a sub-agency, Master for top-level admin access.
  4. Set Active to Yes and click Save Agency.
🚫
Master Flag — Setting Type to "Master" grants full system access. Only assign this to trusted administrators. There should generally be only one Master agency.

Filtering Views by Agency

On the Clients, Properties, Units, Tenants, and Transactions screens, a filter dropdown at the top lets the Master Admin limit the view to records belonging to a specific agency — useful for focused auditing.

Resetting Tenant Credentials

In the Tenants section, a Reset option allows the Master Admin to clear a tenant's username and password, forcing them to go through First Time Setup again on their next login.

7.3 Transaction Verification

Verifying (authenticating) a transaction confirms it is correct and locks it from further edits or deletion. Only the Master Admin can do this.

  1. Click Transactions in the sidebar.
  2. Find the transaction with status Pending.
  3. Click the transaction's Edit / Verify button to open the detail modal.
  4. Review the date, amount, description, and attached document.
  5. Click Verify Transaction (or the Confirm/Authenticate button).
  6. The status changes to Verified. The transaction is now locked.
⚠️
Verification is permanent. Once a transaction is verified it cannot be edited or deleted by anyone — including the Master Admin. Verify only after confirming accuracy.

Deleting Transactions

Only Pending transactions can be deleted. Attempting to delete a verified transaction will show an error toast.

Chapter 8

Bulk Data Uploads

When setting up a new property portfolio, entering units and tenants one by one would be time-consuming. The system supports Excel/CSV bulk uploads for both Units and Tenants.

8.1 General Bulk Upload Workflow

  1. Click the Upload button (Units Upload or Tenants Upload) on the relevant list screen.
  2. Click Download Template to get the correctly formatted spreadsheet.
  3. Open the template and fill in your data, one row per record.
  4. Save as .xlsx, .xls, or .csv.
  5. Back in the app, click Choose File and select your file.
  6. The system validates each row and shows a preview:
    • ✅ Valid rows — highlighted green, ready to import.
    • ❌ Invalid rows — highlighted red with an error message explaining the issue.
  7. Click Import X Record(s) to import only the valid rows.
  8. Fix any errors in the spreadsheet and re-upload to import the previously skipped rows.

Chapter 9

Reports & Data Export

The system provides two dedicated report pages plus in-portal dashboards for on-screen monitoring.

9.1 Full Data Report (report.html)

This is a comprehensive, print-ready report of all data in the system. Open it directly in the browser:

  1. Open report.html in your browser (or navigate to it from the app folder).
  2. The page loads instantly, pulling all data from the browser's local storage.
  3. Review the sections:
    • Agencies — all agency records.
    • Clients — all client records.
    • Properties — all properties with details.
    • Property Units — all units, status, and rent info.
    • Tenants — all tenants across all properties.
    • Transactions — full transaction history with amounts.
    • Summary Statistics — counts, active/inactive breakdown, occupancy.
  4. To print or save as PDF: press Ctrl+P (Windows) or ⌘+P (Mac). Select Save as PDF as the destination.
💡
The report is formatted for printing with colour-coded section headers, automatic page breaks, and print-safe colour adjustments. Use landscape orientation in your print dialog for wider tables.

9.2 Credentials Report (credentials.html)

This page generates a printable list of all user login credentials in the system — useful for distributing access details to new users during onboarding.

  1. Open credentials.html.
  2. The page shows login details for: Master, Agencies, Clients, and Tenants (including role, code, name, username, password, email, and active status).
  3. Print using Ctrl+P / ⌘+P.
🔒
Sensitive Document — The credentials report contains all usernames and passwords in plain text. Treat printed copies as confidential. Distribute only to authorised personnel and shred after use.

9.3 Dashboard Summaries (In-Portal)

Each role's dashboard provides a live overview without leaving the portal:

PortalReport Information Available
TenantBalance, rent due, last 5 transactions
ClientProperty count, occupancy, tenant count, last 10 transactions, running balance
AgencyClient count, all properties/tenants, occupancy, last 10 transactions
MasterSystem-wide totals for all entities, all transactions, global balance

9.4 Printing Any Screen

Any page in the browser can be printed with Ctrl+P. For best results on dashboard/table screens, use landscape orientation and set margins to Minimum in your browser's print dialog.

Chapter 12

Glossary

Active
A status flag (Yes/No) on records. Inactive records are hidden from selection dropdowns but preserved in the database.
Agency
A property management company that manages one or more clients (property owners) and their portfolios on the platform.
Authenticated / Verified
A transaction status indicating that a Master Admin has reviewed and confirmed the entry. Verified transactions are locked from editing or deletion.
Base64
A method of encoding binary files (images, PDFs) as text so they can be stored in the browser's local storage. File attachments in this system are stored as base64.
Bulk Post
A feature that creates one transaction per selected tenant in a single action — useful for month-end rent charges across a whole property.
Client
A property owner whose portfolio is managed by an agency on the platform.
Credit
A positive transaction amount representing money received into an account (e.g. rent paid by tenant).
Debit
A negative transaction amount representing a charge or money owed (e.g. maintenance fee charged to tenant).
First Time Setup
The one-time process for a new user to create their username and password before their first login.
Local Storage
Browser-based data storage used by this application. Data persists between browser sessions but is tied to the specific browser and device.
Master Admin
The top-level administrator with unrestricted access to all data and the exclusive ability to verify transactions, manage agencies, and reset tenant credentials.
Modal
A pop-up form overlay that appears on top of the current page. Used for all add, edit, and view operations.
Pending
The default status for a newly posted transaction, indicating it has not yet been reviewed or verified by a Master Admin.
Property Code
A unique auto-generated identifier for each property (e.g. PR001).
Rent Frequency
How often a tenant pays rent: Monthly, Quarterly (every 3 months), SemiAnnually (every 6 months), or Annually (once per year).
Running Balance
The sum of all transaction amounts for a tenant or portfolio — positive means a net credit, negative means a net debit/outstanding amount.
Session
A temporary login state stored in the browser's session storage. Cleared automatically when the browser tab is closed.
Tenant Number
A unique auto-generated identifier for each tenant (format: TN-###).
Toast
A small, temporary notification banner that appears in the top-right corner of the screen after an action, then disappears automatically.
Transaction Reference
A unique identifier for each transaction (format: TXN-#####). Note this number when submitting a payment for future enquiries.
Unit Code
A unique auto-generated identifier for each property unit (e.g. UN001).
Chapter 13

Troubleshooting

ProblemLikely CauseSolution
Cannot log in — "Invalid credentials" Wrong username, password, or property code (Tenants). Double-check all fields. Note: usernames and passwords are case-sensitive. Use Forgot Password if needed.
Cannot log in — "Account inactive" The account's Active flag is set to No. Contact your Agency or Master Admin to re-activate the account.
First Time Setup: "Phone number does not match" The telephone number entered does not match the one registered by the administrator. Contact your administrator to confirm the exact phone number on file.
Reset code expired More than 15 minutes passed since the reset code was generated. Start the Forgot Password process again to generate a new code.
Delete button is greyed out / disabled The record has dependent data (e.g. a property with active tenants cannot be deleted). Deactivate or remove all dependent records first, then delete.
Cannot edit or delete a transaction The transaction has been verified (status = Verified). Contact your Master Admin. Verified transactions are locked by design.
File upload fails silently File type not supported, or file is very large. Check the accepted file types listed in the upload field. Try a smaller or compressed file.
Bulk upload shows all rows as errors Column headers do not match the template, or the wrong file was uploaded. Always start from the downloaded template. Do not rename or reorder the columns.
Unit dropdown is empty when adding a tenant No units exist for the selected property, or all units are occupied. Add units to the property first, then return to add the tenant.
Data has disappeared after switching browsers or devices Data is stored in browser local storage, which is device and browser-specific. Always use the same browser on the same device. Data does not sync across devices.
Dashboard shows zero records but data was entered Session expired (browser was closed and reopened). Log in again. Your data is still in the browser's local storage.
Report page shows no data Opened report.html in a different browser to where data was entered. Open the report in the same browser where the app data was entered.
"Username already exists" when setting up Another user in the same role has the same username. Choose a different, unique username.

Getting Further Help

If you encounter an issue not listed above:

  • Tenants — contact your Client or Agency administrator.
  • Clients — contact your Agency administrator.
  • Agencies — contact the Master Admin.
  • Master Admin — consult the application's technical documentation or developer.
